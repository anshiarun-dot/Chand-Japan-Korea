✈️ Arrival into Tokyo
  • 🛬 Land at Haneda 10:50pm — long travel day, go easy

Tokyo
Tue
29 Dec
⛩️ Asakusa, Akihabara & Glasses
  • 🌅 Morning: Sensoji Temple, Asakusa — Tokyo's oldest temple, beautiful Nakamise shopping street. Great for souvenirs
  • 🎮 Akihabara — anime, K-pop, multi-floor game arcades, figures, merch. Take your time here
  • 👓 JINS or Zoff glasses MUST DO — both have stores in Akihabara! Pick frames, get lenses cut on the spot. Takes about 30–60 min. Bring your prescription. Incredibly cheap compared to NZ (~¥5,000–15,000 all in)
  • 🛍️ Don Quijote (Donki) in Akihabara for snacks, beauty, random amazing things
  • 🌆 Evening: Tokyo Skytree for city views at night — stunning
💡 JINS: jins.com/jp — find your nearest store. Bring your optometrist prescription from NZ. Single vision lenses are often done same-day.

Tokyo
Wed
30 Dec
🏔️ Mt Fuji Day Trip MUST DO
  • 🚌 Early start — Keio Bus from Shinjuku Bus Terminal to Fuji 5th Station (~2h15m). Book tickets online in advance at highway-buses.jp
  • 🗻 Fuji 5th Station — highest point accessible by road in winter. December = clear skies, stunning views, no crowds. Walk around, buy the famous Fuji walking stick
  • 🌊 Lake Kawaguchiko — the iconic Fuji reflection lake. Walk the shore, find the famous red pagoda photo spot
  • ♨️ Onsen at Kawaguchiko — soak in hot springs with Fuji views. Many options on the lake shore
  • 🍡 Lunch: Hoto noodles — Yamanashi region specialty, thick flat noodles in miso broth with pumpkin. Very veg-adaptable
  • 🚌 Return bus to Shinjuku (~2h15m)
💡 Dress very warmly — 5th Station is 0–5°C in December. Book the bus at least 1–2 weeks ahead, it sells out.

🏎️ Go-Karting, Shibuya & NYE 🎊
  • 🚦 Morning: Shibuya Scramble Crossing — watch from Starbucks upstairs, then join the crossing
  • 🛍️ Harajuku Takeshita Street + Omotesando for a wander and shopping
  • 🏎️ Afternoon: MariCar Street Go-Karting MUST DO BOOK AHEAD — dress as Mario characters, drive through Shinjuku & Shibuya streets. ~¥8,000–12,000/person. Book at maricar.com. Bring your NZ licence + International Driving Permit!
  • 🍣 Fancy Omakase Dinner FANCY DINNER ✨ — your one special meal! NYE at a Tokyo sushi counter is unforgettable. Budget ~¥15,000–25,000/person. Book months ahead via Tableall or Omakase app
  • 🎊 New Year's Eve! — After dinner, Sensoji Temple's Joya no Kane bell ringing at midnight. Or Shibuya/Shinjuku for huge NYE street party vibes
  • 🧳 Tonight: pack your bags — heading to Osaka tomorrow morning!
💡 IDP = International Driving Permit. Get it from AA New Zealand before you leave (~$35). Mandatory for MariCar — they will turn you away without it.

🎍 New Year's Day — Kyoto Stopover → Osaka
  • 🚅 Morning Shinkansen from Tokyo — stop in Kyoto first (store bags at Kyoto Station lockers)
  • 🦊 Fushimi Inari — the famous 10,000 orange torii gates. Only 20 min from Kyoto Station, hike as far as you like. Magical on New Year's Day
  • 🎋 Arashiyama Bamboo Grove — towering bamboo forest, beautiful in winter light
  • 🍵 Matcha tea and wagashi sweets at a Kyoto tea house
  • 🚅 Late afternoon: short train to Osaka (~15 min from Kyoto)
  • 🏨 Check in: APA Hotel & Resort Osaka
  • 🦑 Evening: Dotonbori — Osaka's iconic neon food street. Takoyaki, okonomiyaki, the giant Glico man sign
💡 New Year's Day is actually a great day to travel — Shinkansen runs fully, Kyoto is calm, and Fushimi Inari is stunning with minimal crowds compared to central Kyoto shrines.

🏯 Osaka Castle & Kyoto Day Trip
  • 🏯 Morning: Osaka Castle — stunning hilltop castle, beautiful grounds for a morning walk
  • 🚅 Afternoon day trip to Kyoto (~15 min): Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) + Gion district — preserved geisha quarter, wooden teahouses, Hanamikoji Street at dusk
  • 👘 Rent Kimono in Gion MUST DO — walk the historic lanes in traditional dress. Rental studios from ¥3,000–6,000. Incredible photos against temples and machiya houses
  • 🛍️ Nishiki Market — "Kyoto's Kitchen", covered market with pickles, tofu, matcha everything
  • 🌆 Back to Osaka for dinner: Shinsekai — retro old-school Osaka neighbourhood, amazing street food
💡 Gion is most atmospheric at dusk — time it so you're walking Hanamikoji Street around 5–6pm.

✈️ Arrive Seoul — Myeongdong First Look
  • 🛬 Land at Incheon 4:10pm — take AREX Express train to city (~45 min, ₩9,500). Buy T-money card at airport for all Seoul transport
  • 🏨 Check in: Hyundai Residences Seoul

💡 T-money card works on metro, buses, taxis, and even some convenience stores. Load ₩50,000 to start.

🪖 DMZ Tour + Gyeongbokgung
  • 🪖 DMZ Day Tour MUST DO BOOK AHEAD — the Korean Demilitarized Zone. 3rd Infiltration Tunnel, Dora Observatory, Joint Security Area (JSA) if available. Mostly bus-based, very accessible. Book a guided tour from Seoul (~₩55,000–90,000/person). Depart ~7:30am
  • 🏛️ Afternoon: Gyeongbokgung Palace — Korea's grandest royal palace. Changing of the guard at 2pm
  • 👘 Rent Hanbok MUST DO — traditional Korean dress rental right near the palace (~₩20,000–40,000). Free entry to the palace in Hanbok! Beautiful photos against the palace backdrop
  • 🏘️ Bukchon Hanok Village — traditional village with stunning Seoul skyline + palace views, right next to Gyeongbokgung
💡 DMZ tours book out — reserve as soon as your dates are confirmed. Most reputable ones go through Viator, Klook, or directly via Korea DMZ Tours.

  • 🎵 HYBE Insight Museum BOOK AHEAD — interactive BTS/HYBE label museum. Sells out weeks ahead, reserve online

// Before You Leave Auckland — Action Items
🪪 International Driving Permit Get from AA New Zealand (~$35) before departure. Mandatory for MariCar go-karting — they will turn you away without it. Also bring your NZ licence.
👓 Glasses Prescription Bring your optometrist prescription from NZ. JINS and Zoff in Akihabara can cut lenses same-day for single vision. Have your PD (pupillary distance) measurement too.
🎟️ Book These in Advance MariCar (maricar.com), Mt Fuji Keio Bus (highway-buses.jp), DMZ tour (Klook/Viator), HYBE Insight Museum, omakase dinner. These all sell out.
📋 Visit Japan Web Register all 3 passports at vjw.digital.go.jp before departure. Fast-tracks immigration on arrival in Japan. Takes 10 min, saves significant time.
💴 Cash & Cards Japan: carry JPY cash — temples, small restaurants, taxis often cash only. Withdraw at 7-Eleven or Japan Post ATMs. Korea: more card-friendly but have some KRW on hand.
🚃 Transport Cards Pick up a Suica card (Japan) at Haneda Airport on arrival. Pick up a T-money card (Korea) at Incheon Airport. Both work on trains, buses, taxis. Load ¥5,000 and ₩50,000 respectively to start.
